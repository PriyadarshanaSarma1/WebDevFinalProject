Africa

Cairo, Egypt

Cairo is the capital city of Egypt. It is one of the largest cities in Africa. For many years, Cairo has stood on the banks of Nile. It has served as a gateway to the Nile delta which is located in the northeast of the country. This separates into the Rosetta and Damietta branches.

Cairo's people was one ethnically and religious diverse. Now, Cairo is a moslty muslim country. A number of Egyptian Christians live in various areas of the city, majority of which observe Coptic Orthodox faith. In other areas, old Italian, Greek, Syrian, and Sudanese comminuties are still found. Status differences in the population of Egypt on a person's birth of origin or their modernity degree. Around half of Cairo's population are in the city proper. The other half of the population are in the suburbs.

For a long time in the Middle East, Cairo has been a cultural capital and the chief of mass media centre in the region. Cairo has also been the area for the region's religious and cultural institutions. In the 19th century, the introduction of different European cultutal insitutions took place. Amongst these include theatres such as the Baroque Opera House, found on the Opera Square in downtown Cairo. This was destroyed by fire in 1971 and was later on replaced with a newer structure in 1988.

Lagos, Nigeria

Lagos city is a city and chief port of the Lagos state of Nigeria. It served as the capital of Lagos state until 1975. In December 1991, Lagos city became the federal capital of Nigeria. This has been replaced now. Ikeja became the state capital, and Abuja became the federal capital. However, in many government agencies, Lagoes remained as the unofficial seat.

In Lagos Island is where Lagos' population is found. This is situated on Lagos Lagoon, on the Bight of Benin in the Guld of Guinea. Lagos is known to be Nigeria's largest city. Lagos is also one of the largest sub-Saharan Africa.

In the 15th century, Yoruba fishermen and hunters settled in Lagos Island. They are called Oko. From the late 16th century to mid-19th century, Lagos Island has been taken over by the Kingdown of Benin. They are called Eko.

In 1472, Portuguese people came to Lagos Island. Trade developed around this time. This was halted a century later when the Portuguese were granted asientos de negros-monopolies. This was to sell slaves in the Spanish Americas. Kings (also known as local obas), liked the good relationship between them and the Portuguese. They establoshed a growing slave trade. In 1851, the British attempts to stop the trade. They sent out a naval attack on Lagos and overthew the oba. It was not until 1861 when Lagos was under british control when the slave trade stopped.

Johannesburg, South Africa

Johannesburg is a city in the Gauteng province of South Africa. Johannesburg is the country's chief industrial and financial capital. In 1886 was when Johannesburg was first founded. This was followed by the discovery of gold. Initially, Johannesburg was a part of the Transvaal republic. Transvaal is an independent Afrikaner, or Boer, which became part of the four provinces of South Africa. Now, Johannesburg is part of the Gauteng. Gauteng is one of the nine provinces of South Africa.

Johannesburg's division is reflected from a century of racially driven social engineering, racial segragation in South Africa between 1948 to 1994. This resulted in a city of contrast. That is, area of abundance and richness on one side, and an area of poverty on the other.

Johannesburg has a rich cultural life. You have abundance in arts. Johannesburg is has a sunny climate. People spend a lot of time outdoors. In the northen part of the city, there is a broad space for bird-watching and picniking. People here are also avid sportsmen. After the return from political isolation, Johannesburg hosted different international competitions. Among these include rugby, football, cricket, golf, and tennis.

Asia

Seoul, South Korea

Seoul is a city and capital of South Korea, also known as the Republic of Korea. Seoul is located on the northwestern part of the country, situated on the Han River.

Seoul was initially the capital of Korea until 1984 when the formal division of the country took place. Some of the official names of Seoul included Hansŏng (Hanseong), Kyŏngsŏng (Gyeongseong), Hanyang, but the city was famously called Seoul during both the Chosŏn (Yi) dynasty and during the Japanese rule.

Seoul is the South Korean's cultural centre. It is the home of many national academies including National Academy of Arts, the National Academy of Sciences, National Classical Music Institute. Seoul is also home to almost all of the South Korea's societies and libraries.

Seoul has numerous parks, both small and large. There are many places of historical spots. Amongst this include Ch’anggyŏng (Changgyeong), Kyŏngbok, Tŏksu (Deoksu), and Ch’angdŏk (Changdeok) palaces as well as Chong-myo (Jongmyo).

Seoul is also has excellent sports and recreational facilities. Amongst this Seoul Sports Complex situtated south of Han, Olympic Park situated to the east of the Sports Complex, an Olympics museum, the Korean National University of Physical Education, and an earthen fortress from the early Paekche (Baekje) period.

Singapore, Singapore

Singapore is a city-state in the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula. Singapore consists of the diamond-shaped Singapore Island. There are also some 60 small islets. Originally, the Singapore Island was occupied by fishermen and pirates. The area served as an outpost for the Sumatran empire of Srīvijaya.

Singaporean cultural activities, practiced by many cultural societies and professional groups within the city, are products from other major civilizations of China, India, Indonesia, or the West. This includes traditional Chinese and Indian music, painting, and drama. There is also, popular culture which is based on modern mass media. This includes Malay music, which was from rhythms of Western orchestras, musical films that popularize Hindi and Tamil songs, as do films from Hong Kong, Taiwan, and the United States.

Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo is the capital and largest city of Japan. It has three prefectures bordering it—Saitama on the north, Chiba on the east, and Kanagawa on the south—may.

Tokyo is the central of Japanese culture. Greater Tokyo has a third of the universities in the entire country. A big part of the societies, insitutes, and libraries, are found here. A lot writers live in Tokyo. Tokyo also has the main museums compared to any other Japanese city. The same thing can be said for theatres and concert halls. Important cultural institutions such as the Tokyo National Museum, National Diet Library, National Theatre, and Tokyo Metropolitan Art Museum are found near national government offices or in Ueno.

London, United Kingdom

London is the city capital of the United Kingdom. London is one of the oldest great cities of the world. London is also the country’s economic, transportation, and cultural centre.

London has about a third of the country's art galleries. Some of the greatest collections is the National Gallery. This is found in the Trafalgar Square. Behind this is where one can find the National Portrait Gallery. This is place has a large collection of paintings, drawings, sculptures, etchings, photographs, and miniatures of famous faces past and present.

People here are big fans of football. Football is at the top of the list, both in participang and spectator sport in London. You will see hundreds of amateur players for games in every park and open space. London has some dozen football clubs. This including Arsenal, Chelsea, Tottenham Hotspur, and West Ham United.

Paris is the city and capital of France. Paris is located in the north-central part of the France.

For several centuries, Paris has been the cultural caputal of the Western world. It has been a known to many artists, intellectuals, and a place where new ideas originate. Art also reign supreme. This was most expecially true during the 20th century. This wa during the time when numerous writers, artists, including Ernest Hemingway from the United States, James Joyce from Ireland, Pablo Picasso from Spain, and Amedeo Modigliani from Italy.

Madrid, Spain

Madrid is a city and capital of Spain. Madrid is also, Spain’s arts and financial centre. It is considered to be the city proper and has formed an autonomous community in central Spain.

Madrid has many attractions. It has the largest bullring in Spain, known as the Las Ventas. This place as a capacity of 25,000 people and is the place where novice bullfighters have to display their skills in the alternativa. This is the event where a matador kills his first bull. Bullfighting lasts from March to October.

Football is also a big sport here. There are two major football teams in Madrid known as the Real Madrid and Club Atlético de Madrid. Some of the important football event is the annual matches against the Barcelona squads.

North America

Los Angeles, United States

Los Angeles is the city and seat of Los Angeles county of southern California, U.S. Los Angeles is the second most populated city in the United States. The city is found across a broad coastal plain. The city is between mountains and the Pacific Ocean. The city is where you can find the areas of Beverly Hills, Pasadena, and Long Beach. This is also where Channel Islands, Santa Catalina and San Clemente are found.

Los Angeles is the central of southern Califonia. Just recently, it became a world-class city. During the 20th century, it was just considered as a "large village". It overcame it's natural lacks and became an establishment of commerce, agriculture, tourism, and industry. It became known to be associated to good weather, leisure and recreation, and especially the aura of Hollywood. However, it does have exceptions. Los Angeles has been a place of ethnic and racial diversity, largely due to immigration, and shows a growing gap between rich and poor.

New York, United States

New York City is the largest and most influential city in the United States. This city covers Manhattan and Staten islands, the western sections of Long Island, and a part of the New York state mainland to the north of Manhattan.

New York City composes of many neighbourhoods. This is known as the fire boroughs. This includes Manhattan, Brooklyn, the Bronx, Queens, and Staten Island.

During the end of the 20th century, there were roughly 200 national groups in the city. This includes European ancestry, Hispanics, African Americans, and Asians, which is now the fastest-growing component of the population. The Statue of Liberty continues to symbolize enligtenment, as it welcomes immigrants into the city's golden door.

Toronto, Canada

Toronto is the city and capital of the province of Ontario, Canada. Toronto is the most populated city in Canada. It is a multicultural city, and considered as the country's commercial and financial centre.

Toronto has a continental climate. Due to its distance from the Great Lakes, this changes. During winter, average temperature is in the low to mid-20s F (about –4.2 °C), but wind chill may lower this even more. In the summer months, temperature can go from low 70s F (about 22.2 °C) to 90 °F (32 °C). The Great Lakes also affects precipitation which can cause it to rain all year round.

Toronto is a cultural centre. There are plenty of musical groups that are known internationally. For example, the Toronto Symphony Orchestra. There are also many major theatres in the area including Factory Theatre. There are also plenty of museums, such as the The Art Gallery of Ontario and the Royal Ontario Museum. Different attraction destinations also exist in this city such as the Ontario Science Centre, which holds plenty of hands-on exhibists, and Ontario Place, which holds the Canadian National Exhibition yearly.

South America

Lima, Peru

Lima is the city and capital of Peru. Lima is considered as Peru's commercial and industrial centre. Lima's nickname is El Pulpo (“The Octopus”). Lima is the a fourth of the total population of Peru. This is a result of concentration of people, capital, political influence, and social innovations. For a long time, Lima was the only point of contact between the country and other countries.

Lima's city layout is strongly influenced by its long history. The checked street layout comes from Spanish colonists during the 16th century. Lima also has many colonial buildings. For example, The Presidential Palacen and many more reflect the past popularity of the French Empire style.

Lima's population has transformed since the 1930s. It is now hard to find a true Limeño. Everywhere, one can hear different tones, accents, which reflects different origins who have come to this city. Before arrival of migrants, it was easy to tell between European and other ethnic mixtures. Modern-day Lima, however, classification is no longer easy. Currently, the only classification that persists is rich and influencial versus poor and powerless.

In spite problems that face Lima and those those who reside in the city, it is still the dominant and most vibrant cultural centre of Peru.

Montevideo, Uruguay

Montevideo city is the principal city and capital of Uruguay. It found on the north shore of the Río de la Plata estuary.

In 1726, Montevideo was founded by Bruno Mauricio de Zabala. During this time, Montevideo was mostly a Spanish town. During its early years, Montevideo was mostly a Spanish garrison town. As trade expanded, Uruguay gained more independece.

During the early 1800s, Montevideo was occupied by British, Spanish, Argentine, Portuguese, and Brazilian forces. During this time, trade and population declined. This lasted for years, but it was not until the Montevideo succeeded the in 1851 when Montevideo became the major port of the Río de la Plata, gaining the power back.

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Rio de Janeiro is the city and port capital Brazil. Rio de Janeiro is located on the Atlantic Ocean. This is city is known to be amongst the world's most beautiful and most interesting urban centres.

Rio de Janeiro's name was given by Portuguese navigators. They arrived to the city in 1502 and mistakenly thought the entrace of the bay as the month of a river. In Portuguese, rio is the word for river.

Rio de Janeiro is known for its beautiful beaches, peaks of ridges, and hills covered by tropical forests. Rio de Janeiro is a centre of leasure not just for Brazilian people, but also foreign tourists as well.

Rio de Janeiro is an economic centre. The activity ranges from inductry, trade, administration, banking, education, culture, and research.
