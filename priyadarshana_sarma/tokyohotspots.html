Tokyo

Sensō-ji is an ancient Buddhist temple located in Asakusa, Tokyo. Sensoji Temple is a few steps from Asakusa Station. It is Tokyo's oldest temple, and one of its most significant temples. The temple’s principal image is the Sacred Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva, who is known for being the most compassionate, and for relieving suffering and answering prayers with great benevolence. The Sensoji Kannon temple is dedicated to Kannon Bosatsu, the Bodhisattva of compassion, and is the most widely visited spiritual site in the world with over 30 million visitors annually. Tourists can visit the temple in the month of May to experience the Sanja Matsuri, the annual festival of the Asakusa Shrine.

Tokyo Skytree is a broadcasting and observation tower in Sumida, Tokyo. It became the tallest structure in Japan in 2010 and reached its full height of 634.0 meters (2,080 ft) in March 2011. It is the centerpiece of the Tokyo Skytree Town in the Sumida City Ward, not far away from Asakusa. The highlight of the Tokyo Skytree is its two observation decks which offer spectacular views out over Tokyo. The two enclosed decks are located at heights of 350 and 450 meters respectively, making them the highest observation decks in Japan and some of the highest in the world. A large shopping complex with aquarium is located at the base of the Skytree.

Ueno park is a specious park in Ueno district, Tokyo. Its one of the oldest parks in the country, and was founded based on assimilating international practices in the early Meji period. A tourist can visit and enjoy many of the museums there. But the best time to visit is during Spring, where it is home to a spectacular bloom of cherry blossoms. With the bloom, there is also the Hanami festival that takes places, which is always a delight to enjoy. Millions of people visit the park every year, making it the country's most visited park.
